Capital Beginning to Flow in Advance of BEAD Allocations

  • August 19, 2024
  • 6:00 am

SHARE THIS ARTICLE

Fiber providers are securing capital for their deployment of rural fiber networks in addition to the government’s $42.45 billion BEAD Program. Along with letters of credit, rural broadband providers that receive federal or state subsidies are required to have 25 percent in matching funds by the NTIA, according to the Notice of Funding Opportunity.

Nextlink Internet, which provides internet service across Texas, Oklahoma, Kansas, Nebraska, Iowa, and Illinois, has announced an additional $100+ million investment from Cable One and CanAm in equity and debt capital for further rural fiber deployments. The additional capital infusions will speed up deployment of fiber in Texas, serving an additional 50,000+ rural and small-town homes and businesses with speeds up to 5 Gbps symmetrical. 

“We are already in permitting discussions with many of the communities we seek to serve and expect to have field crews actively working on building these fiber networks in the coming weeks,” said Nextlink Internet CEO Bill Baker. “Additionally, this capital raise serves to further strengthen our balance sheet as Nextlink looks to participate in the upcoming BEAD program.”

John Reid, Head of Investment Finance at CanAm, said, “This closing marks another milestone for CanAm as we continue to make substantial capital investment in the rural telecom infrastructure space.”

Another BEAD hopeful, Brightspeed, which provides DSL, fixed wireless, and fiber internet to more than 1.3 million locations across 20 states, has received approximately $3.7 billion in new capital from its financial stakeholders, including its secured lenders and funds managed by Apollo Global Management. This money secures Brightspeed’s ability to pursue access to additional funding through the BEAD Program, of which approximately $4.7 billion is available across the company’s footprint.

“This landmark announcement builds on our strong momentum and provides the capital to advance our aggressive, multi-year plan to build a fiber network in communities that lack quality, high-speed connectivity options,” said Brightspeed CEO Tom Maguire.  

Brightspeed is pursuing state and federal grant funding to augment its current build plan to help close the digital divide, in addition to multi-million dollar investments in its fiber broadband network to new communities across its footprint.

By J. Sharpe Smith, Inside Towers Technology Editor
